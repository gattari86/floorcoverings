1. Forensic Analysis of Late 2025 Traffic Drop

1.1 The September 2025 Reporting Glitch

The most immediate cause: Google disabled the &num=100 parameter and filtered bot traffic from Search Console reporting.

What This Means: Rank-tracking software like SEMrush, Ahrefs, and Moz were generating thousands of "fake" impressions by crawling pages looking for specific keywords. When these bots disappeared, impressions dropped 40%, but zero real customers were lost.

1.2 October & November 2025 Core Updates

Google released two aggressive algorithmic updates that fundamentally shifted local search:

The Shift: From Relevance → Popularity

  • Old Model (Pre-2025): A business ranked by optimizing keywords and on-page content. Static optimization = reliable results.
  • New Model (2025+): A business ranks by proving it's "alive" and engaging with customers. Active velocity of reviews, fresh content, and user interactions matter more than historical reputation.
Implication for FCI: Even with a stellar 4.85★ rating, if competitors like Roberts Carpet are generating 2-3 reviews per week while FCI averages 1-2 per month, the algorithm will favor Roberts for "local pack" visibility.
